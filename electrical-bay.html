Custom VTOL Aircraft Electrical Bay

Figure 1: Final installed electrical bay in use in the field

CU Air's Artemis Electrical Bay (ebay) serves the purpose of keeping batteries and electronics secure during flight.

This project gave me experience with designing at the interface of multiple subteams. It presented the unique challenge of tailoring my design to the varying needs of multiple different groups within CU Air. The electrical subteam needed the components to be easily accessible. The autopilot subteam needed certain components such as the Pixhawk to be placed far from batteries to prevent interference. The mechanical teams needed the ebay to be lightweight and easy to work with when replacing batteries. As I worked through these problems I also adapted to the rapidly changing needs of the team, resulting in multple iterations shown below.

System Contents

The ebay held the following components:
  • 6x Flight Batteries (Tattu 6S 8000mAh)
  • Flight Electronics Battery
  • Pixhawk
  • Rocket
  • Raspberry Pi
  • Flight Validation Board
  • Gimbal Board
  • Power Monitoring System
  • R10C Current Sensing Board
  • Pixhawk Breakout Board
  • Power Distribution Board
  • Airdrop Board
  • Video Capture Board
  • General Development Board/Debug Board (for detecting issues before flight)

System Requirements

Functional Requirements:
  • FR-1: Must securely hold 6x 6S 8000 mAh batteries and 1x small batteries.
  • FR-2: Must securely hold all 10 boards.
  • FR-3: Must provide adequate distance or insulation between the Pixhawk and batteries.
  • FR-4: Must provide consistently level mounting for the Pixhawk.
  • FR-5: Must separate necessary components to prevent battery interference.
  • FR-6: Must provide adequate ventilation.
  • FR-7: Must constrain the bulkhead vertically.
Non-Functional Requirements:
  • NFR-1: The Ebay shall be designed so that one person can exchange the batteries within 5 minutes (including plugging in and unplugging power wires)
  • NFR-2: The V4 Ebay mass shall not exceed V3 Mass.
  • NFR-3: The Ebay shall allow easy visibility for all electronic components so that members can validate their systems before flight.
  • NFR-4: The Ebay shall remain functional even in the case of a unexpected drop or a 360 degree roll.

Board Placement and Wire Management

Figure 4 and 5: Annotated diagrams of Board Placements.

Figure 6: Diagram of wire paths.

In the final design, the boards are mostly secured with screws to the wooden piece with the exception of the rocket. The wires to the rocket are too stiff to move freely, so the rocket is velcroed to the top of the fuselage.

The wires were difficult to organize in such a small space, but the most important aspect for wire management was keeping the wires out of the battery section and out of the way of the airdrop and baydoors. This was accomplished by the wall towards the back of the fuselage. Power Wires were secured with velcro loops and zip ties if they had to cross into the airdrop area.

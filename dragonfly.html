Dragonfly — VTOL Tilt-Rotor Plane

Figure 1: Dragonfly in progress

Dragonfly is a VTOL (Vertical Takeoff and Landing) tilt-rotor plane I am designing and building from scratch in collaboration with my friend Charles Yan. The project began this past summer as a personal challenge to push beyond the structured environment of project teams and take full ownership of an aircraft — from concept to first flight.

The tilt-rotor configuration was chosen for its versatility: the ability to take off and land vertically like a quadcopter while transitioning to efficient fixed-wing cruise flight. Every aspect of the aircraft — airframe, propulsion, electronics, and control systems — is being designed and built by the two of us. We are currently targeting a first flight date of March 27, 2026.

Design and Build Process

The design process has involved iterating through airframe geometry, motor placement, and control surface sizing to achieve stable flight in both VTOL and fixed-wing modes. Key aspects of the project include:
  • Custom airframe design optimized for tilt-rotor transition flight
  • Motor and propeller selection for hover efficiency and forward thrust
  • Flight controller configuration and tuning for dual-mode operation
  • Lightweight structural construction to maximize thrust-to-weight ratio
